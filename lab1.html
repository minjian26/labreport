Introduction

In mathematics, a fractal is a geometric shape containing detailed structure at arbitrarily small scales, usually having a fractal dimension strictly exceeding the topological dimension. Many fractals appear to have their similar shapes at various scales. Their patterns never end. The characteristic of fractals is self-similarity. When zoomed in or out, they display their similar patterns or shapes at many magnification levels. A classic example of a self-similar fractal is the Sierpinski triangle, where smaller triangles within the larger triangle are identical.
